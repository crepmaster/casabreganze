Milan in summer has a misleading reputation: "business city, everyone leaves." That's precisely when it becomes most endearing. When the crowds desert, the city reinvents itself: you swim in a Liberty-style pool from the 1930s, listen to young singers with their feet at water's edge, rediscover historic villas by candlelight. Here's our selection of truly verified experiences for this summer, far from expected postcards — all verified, with dates, locations and sources at the end of the article.

## Swimming in an Art Deco Gem: Bagni Misteriosi

If you had to choose just one place, it would be this one. The former restored Caimi pool integrated into the cultural hub of Teatro Franco Parenti, Bagni Misteriosi is Milan's most "Fellinian" spot: retro curves, Art Deco charm and Spritz by the poolside. The 2026 swimming season opens from June 1st, and bathing at Bagni Misteriosi... the summer season will be open until September 10th.

For rates, expect for pool access (Via Carlo Botta 18a):
- 25 euros for the full day (10-18:30) and 15 euros for morning (10-14) or afternoon (14:30-18:30) slots
- reduced rates valid for under 12, over 65, ISEE ≤ €18,000 and Government Agencies

What makes the place unique isn't just the baths. In the evening, the pool becomes a stage. From June 17 to July 22, 2026, the È tempo di Indie summer music festival returns to Bagni Misteriosi in Milan (Via Carlo Botta 18a), dedicated to Italy's new independent scene. For six Wednesday evenings, poolside, twelve emerging artists alternate in concert. The outdoor pool in Porta Romana becomes the stage for acoustic concerts from Italy's indie scene. Admission is 15 euros and shows begin at 8:30 PM. A singular and intimate evening, to be savored until July 22.

## Secret Villas by Candlelight: FAI Summer Evenings

Perhaps the most poetic experience — and the least known to passing tourists. Concerts and theatrical performances, outdoor cinema festivals, aperitivos and dinners under the stars, special candlelit tours and astronomical observations: 9 properties of FAI – Fondo per l'Ambiente Italiano ETS in Lombardy open their doors after sunset.

A few confirmed appointments to note:
- In the heart of Milan: Villa Necchi Campiglio opens its gates on the evenings of July 15, 22 and 29 and August 26 with "All'ombra delle magnolie": poolside aperitivo, candles and swing music.
- On Lake Como: Villa del Balbianello... Sunset aperitivo August 9, September 13, 2026... to enjoy the sunset light... accompanied by an aperitivo in Loggia Segrè and background music.
- In Varese: Villa Panza... Sunset guided tour July 18, August 1, 2026... the villa will be open by reservation for an exclusive and reserved guided tour.

Villa Necchi Campiglio, in particular, is just minutes from our EasyRest apartment: from the center, you can easily reach these hushed evenings on foot or by public transport, before returning home to rest in peace.

## Castello Sforzesco Transforms into Open-Air Theater

The Cortile delle Armi (courtyard of arms) of the castle becomes one of the city's most magical places each summer. The Cortile delle Armi of Castello Sforzesco will host the 14th edition of Estate al Castello, the festival promoted by the City of Milan... We're talking about over 40 events including theatrical performances, concerts, meetings, dance and even cinema. Estate al Castello festival... (from June 22nd to September 11th) with concerts, dance performances, theatre and live shows.

What makes it singular is the mix of genres: concerts, dance, but also meetings with thinkers and science communicators. This year's guests also include artists of the caliber of Alex Britti, Angelique Kidjo, Noa, Sal Da Vinci and Frankie hi-nrg mc, as well as guests like Massimo Recalcati, Umberto Galimberti and Mario Tozzi.

## A Pink Night by the Water: Notte Rosa at Idroscalo

Idroscalo — Milan's "sea" — hosts one of the most anticipated free festivals of summer. From July 10-12, Notte Rosa Milano returns to Idroscalo: three free evenings of '90s music, Cartoon Party and vintage market.

Three evenings, three truly atypical vibes:
- Saturday July 11 will be the turn of the Cartoon Party Festival, an event dedicated to the world of cartoons, anime and opening sequences that accompanied the childhood of millions of people.
- Sunday July 12 the festival concludes with the Remira Vintage Market... Over fifty exhibitors will offer vintage clothing, vinyl records, crafts, handmade items and collectibles.

## Open-Air Cinema in the Most Beautiful Cloisters: AriAnteo

Watching a film under the stars in a Milanese cloister is a unique experience. The 2026 edition is the thirty-seventh and runs from June 1 to September 27. Four locations are active, with staggered openings throughout summer. The charm lies in the venues: Four locations in the heart of Milan, a traveling van that brings cinema to neighborhood squares, and programming ranging from world premieres to Kubrick classics.

And good news for the budget: Cinema Revolution... features tickets at the extraordinary price of €3.50... in the period from June 19 to September 20, Cinema Revolution, 3 months of Italian and European cinema at €3.50.

## Urban Art Invades Neighborhoods: GET UP Festival

To venture off the beaten path and discover creative Milan in the periphery, head to District 6. "GET UP – Festival delle Arti Urbane", scheduled from July 11 to September 19, 2026. The project involves various spaces in District 6, including Parco La Spezia, Teatro Puntozero Beccaria, Ecoteatro, Spazio 600 and Don Orione Social Café. It's a participatory festival dedicated to rap, urban dance, theater and visual arts — an opportunity to see the city in an authentic and youthful light.

## Back to the Age of Dinosaurs: Jurassic World: The Experience

For an unusual outing (and perfect for families), Milan is hosting an Italian premiere. From June 27, 2026, Milan hosts for the first time in Italy Jurassic World: The Experience, an immersive journey that brings visitors among animated dinosaurs, spectacular scenography and settings inspired by the famous film saga set on the island of Isla Nublar. Exhibition location: MilanoSesto, Viale Italia 576, Sesto San Giovanni (MI) – Opens to the public Saturday June 27, 2026, and open to the public from June 27, 2026 to January 10, 2027.
